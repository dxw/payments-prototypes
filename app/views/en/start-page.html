Guidance

Claim a Land Transaction Tax refund if your property is derelict or unsuitable for use as a dwelling

Use this online form to amend a Land Transaction Tax (LTT) return to claim a refund if your property is derelict or unsuitable for use as a dwelling.

You can only amend your return within 12 months of the filing date on your original return.

You can make an amendment or claim if you’re:

  • the buyer of the property
  • the solicitor or conveyancer who acted for the buyer in the original purchase
  • someone else authorised to act for the buyer

It usually takes 15 to 20 working days to process your claim. This may take longer if we need more information.

We check all amendments/claims. You'll need to repay the tax and any penalties and interest if it’s wrong. As a result, you may find yourself financially worse off.

Before you start

What you’ll need

  • Your details.
  • The lead buyer’s details if they’re different to your own.
  • Details of the original transaction.
  • 12 digit unique transaction reference number (UTRN).
  • The amount of tax originally paid.
  • The amount of tax to refund.
  • Bank account and sort code details of the person to receive the payment.

Evidence that the property is unsuitable for use as a dwelling or derelict.

You'll need to upload electronic copies during your claim/amendment, about the property, that will show:

  • how your property is unsuitable for use as a dwelling or derelict
  • the condition of the property
  • any repair work that's required

You’ll need to provide electronic copies to upload during your claim/amendment,showing how your property is unsuitable for use as a dwelling or derelict.

You will not be able to complete the form without providing evidence. Contact us if you need to submit a claim or amendment without supporting evidence.

Recommended evidence

  • Royal Institute of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) Homebuyer report, surveyors
    • report, structural report, or building inspector report.
  • Photographs of the property’s condition.
  • Sales particulars.
    • You can also provide additional evidence, such as:
  • contract of sale
  • planning permission
  • invoices and receipts for work completed
  • Council Tax exemption form

We cannot accept:

  • Photographs taken after the effective date (after repair work has started).
  • Blurred, grainy or pixelated images.
