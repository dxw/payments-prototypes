Writing about these prototypes

Single view

In our second sprint, we concentrated on refining the single view idea.

With version two, we prototype three most common complex caes:

  1. Unpaid invoices
  2. Paying in instalments
  3. Amended tax to pay

Preview and send PDF

The biggest change we made from version one, was giving detail to how a taxpayer might be sent their balance. Currently staff type out emails with balance details. So we prototyped what a more consistent and quicker version of this could be.

At the bottom of the page, staff can both enter someone’s email to send a PDF or preview the document before sending.

The PDF is formatted as an A4 letter over multiple pages, including the same information as the digital single view, plus with WRA contact information and how to pay.

Bye bye individual branding

In the first iteration of the single view, we had a novel name and brand - Single Taxpayer View. The intent was to communicate internally that whatever we were building, would bring all information about how much someone ones, into one view.

This then gave way to having default WRA branding, like all other internal applications. As we started to make the app into something to test with staff, it needed to look more realistic.

At this point we still didn’t have a name for the service. We decided to be led by user research and see what names emerged in testing the prototype with staff with the language they used.

Toning down colour use

We began to reduce how much colour we used in the prototype. Starting with making more components grey. Beginning with the login subheader and boxes that highlights why a case should be looked at.

Refining subtotal tables

We continued to refine the subtotal table. Making it clear people could reveal more detail with ‘view’ and close button for the three sections.

Like other components, we replace much of the colour with grey. Using just a thin left border for each expanded row.

Still at this point, everything that makes up someone’s balance is in one subtotal table. Tax, interest, penalties, payments. You name it.
